Cost Per Click Overview

  • Advertisers compete for keywords users will likely use to search for their product/service.
  • These ads are known as "sponsored links" and appear next to organic search results.
  • The three top Pay Per Click companies:

Types of Cost Per Click Services

  • Keyword: Advertisers bid on words or phrases. Results are returned to the user in the order of the highest bidder. This is the most popular and highly marketed form of CPC.
  • Product: Online retailers provide "feeds" of their product database. Users are provided results to companies offering a particular product in relevance to the highest bidder.
    • BizRate.com
    • Shopzilla.com
    • NexTag
    • PriceGrabber.com
    • Shopping.com

Types of Cost Per Click Services

  • Service: Similar to Products, "feeds" of service databases are provided. Users are provided results to companies offering a particular service in relevance to the highest bidder.
    • TripAdvisor
    • SideStep
    • NexTag
  • Pay Per Call: Directories that allow publishers to generate revenue on a Pay Per Call Basis. (similiar to pay per click)

Google AdWords

  • Launched in 2000. Currently Google's flagship product, and main source of revenue.
  • No minimum spending requirement.
  • You're charged only if someone clicks your ad, not when your ad is displayed.
  • The Click Through Rate (CTR) can yield better results than the highest bidder.
  • Offers site targeting that allows you to choose sites in the Google network for your ads to appear on.
  • Google displays ads on partner web sites such as AOL.
  • Google recommends relevant sites within their content network.
  • Provides both UI and Web service API front end to advertisers.

Google AdWords

  • Google doesn't tell you how much you will pay per click. Thus, if you bid $3.00 per click, you will pay anywhere from $0.05 to $3.00 per click.
  • Google does not allow you to know how much your competitors are bidding per click.
    • Google's maximum bid is $100.00
    • Google's minimum bid is $0.05
  • Google targeting system/interface is very simple, efficient, and effective.

Yahoo! Search Marketing

  • in 1998, Goto.com began the first CPC service; renamed Overture in 2001.
  • Yahoo! acquired Overture in 2003 for $1.7 billion.
  • Features Geo-targeting, Ad Testing, Campaign Budgeting, and Campaign scheduling.
  • Each account requires a non-refundable $5 initial deposit
  • Minimum bid requirement of $0.10 per click.

Yahoo! Search Marketing

  • Yahoo allows you to see who you are bidding against and what they are bidding
  • The amount you spend depends on your daily budget and your maximum bid (amount per click).
  • Yahoo places your bid at 1 penny over your next lowest competitor. If you bid $5.00 per click, and the next highest bid is $3.95 per click, you will only pay $3.96 per click.
  • Yahoo allows you to see who you are bidding against and what they are bidding, so you know exactly where you will rank, and how much you will pay.
    • Yahoo's maximum bid is $999.99
    • Yahoo's minimum bid is $0.10
  • Yahoo's targeting system/interface is sub-par and not very user friendly.

MSN adCenter

  • Until the beginning of 2006, all of the ads were supplied by Overture/Yahoo!.
  • As of June 2006, Microsoft is displaying only ads from adCenter.
  • Uses built-in tools to get keyword ideas, audience information, and detailed reports on your ads.
  • See when your clicks lead to sales.
  • Provides both UI and Web service API front end to advertisers.

Conclusion

  • The biggest difference between the major players is the ranking system and how you pay per click.
  • With Yahoo!, you know exactly what you are paying for. Google does not reveal the exact amount you are paying per click.
  • Google yields more page views - increasing your chance of a click though.
  • Yahoo! displays results incrementally according to bid. Google rewards advertisers with rank for better Click Though Rates(CTR).
  • Ultimately, You will pay less per click with Yahoo, but have better results using Google.
